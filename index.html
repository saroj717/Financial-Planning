Nasdaq-100 Historical Annual Returns (1985-2024)

The Nasdaq 100 index (QQQM) is a technology-focused stock index that generated a 15% – 17% average return over the past 38 years, resulting in a cumulative return of over 13, 360.74% including reinvestment of all cash distributions (Dividends). This means investing $1000 every month in the NASDAQ 100 from its inception in January 1985 until February 2024 (a period of 39 years and 1 month) would have grown to approximately $65,897,944.11, given an average annual return rate of 17.74%.
This calculation illustrates the significant impact of long-term compound growth with a high average annual return, even without additional recurring investments. It's a powerful example of how a one-time investment can grow over a long period, especially in an index with strong historical performance like the NASDAQ 100.

When Markets Dip, Don't Drop Out

While staying the course may be hard on your nerves, it can be healthier for your portfolio.An extreme downturn in the market can shake your confidence, but resisting the urge to flinch can pay off.
Bottom line
Bad markets can definitely cause big feelings, but it's generally not a great idea to let emotions take control of your portfolio. A better approach would be to make a plan you can stick to no matter what the market's doing. You can absolutely modify those plans as your goals or situation change, but such adjustments should occur without the influence of any temporary gut feelings. Otherwise, the consequences could be with you for life.

S&P 500 Historical Annual Returns (1985-2023)

The Standard and Poor's 500, or simply the S&P 500, is a stock market index tracking the stock performance of 500 of the largest companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States. The index has generated a 10% - 11% average return over the past 38 years, resulting in a cumulative return of over 6,202% including reinvestment of all cash distributions (Dividends). This means If you had invested $100 in January 1985, your portfolio would have grown to over $6,202 by the end of December 2023. But if you had invested $100 each month since its inception, your portfolio would have grown to over $715, 941, which is Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 25.62 percent.
